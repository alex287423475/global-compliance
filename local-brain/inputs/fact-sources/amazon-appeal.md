# Amazon Appeal Fact Source Pack

This is a sanitized source pack for Local Brain fact-source generation.

Scenario:

- Marketplace: Amazon
- Trigger: Account or listing appeal after a performance notice
- Business context: Cross-border seller needs a POA-style appeal narrative and supporting evidence checklist
- Use boundary: This source pack is for operational document preparation and risk-language review, not legal advice

Source facts to preserve:

- A credible appeal should distinguish root cause, corrective action, and preventive control.
- Evidence should be tied to the specific notice or affected listing.
- The appeal should not blame the platform, promise guaranteed reinstatement, or claim the seller did nothing wrong.
- The supporting file set may include performance notice screenshots, listing screenshots, supplier invoices, inspection records, corrective-action logs, customer-service records, and updated SOP files.
- Human review is required before final submission because only the seller can confirm the factual timeline, supplier records, and account-specific notice details.

Before / after examples:

| Risky expression | Possible reviewer reading | Safer replacement |
| --- | --- | --- |
| We did nothing wrong | The seller has not accepted root-cause responsibility | We identified the process gap that allowed the issue to occur |
| Amazon system mistake | The seller is blaming the platform without evidence | The notice appears to relate to the following listing and documentation gap |
| Guaranteed reinstatement | Uncontrolled outcome promise | Review-ready appeal package with evidence-backed corrective actions |

Human confirmation boundary:

- Confirm the exact notice wording.
- Confirm dates and affected ASINs or listings.
- Confirm supplier invoice authenticity.
- Confirm whether corrective actions have actually been implemented.
- Confirm whether any customer-service statements conflict with the appeal narrative.
